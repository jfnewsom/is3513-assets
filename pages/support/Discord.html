This course has a dedicated Discord server for collaboration, peer support, and community. Participation is entirely optional — no course content or grading depends on it. Before joining, you must read and understand the following terms.

What Discord Is For
✓ Quick Q&A and peer-to-peer help
✓ Lab troubleshooting with classmates
✓ Networking with current students and alumni
✓ Informal discussion about cybersecurity topics
✓ Community building and mentorship
What Discord Is NOT For
✗ Assignment submissions (Canvas only)
✗ Grade discussions or appeals
✗ Deadline extensions or accommodations
✗ Private academic matters
✗ Anything that belongs in an email

UTSA cannot guarantee the security, privacy, or availability of any content shared on this server. Your participation is entirely voluntary and at your own discretion.

Server Rules & Expectations
1. Protect Your Privacy. Use a nickname or first name only. Do not share your student ID, full name, phone number, address, or other personally identifiable information.
2. Keep Academic Records Off Discord. Do not post screenshots of grades, Canvas notifications, or instructor feedback. These conversations happen in email or office hours.
3. Ask in Channels, Not DMs. Questions asked publicly help everyone. Minimize direct messages — if you have a question, others probably do too.
4. Be Respectful — Zero Tolerance for Toxicity. This is a professional learning community, not a complaint forum. Treat your instructor, TAs, classmates, and alumni with respect at all times. This includes:
  • No personal attacks, insults, harassment, or bullying of any kind
  • No passive-aggressive comments, sarcasm directed at individuals, or hostile tone
  • No public venting about grades, deadlines, or course policies — take it to email
  • No @mentioning the instructor to demand responses or escalate complaints
  • Disagreements happen; handle them like a professional or walk away
If you create a hostile environment for others, you will be removed immediately and permanently. There is no warning for this.
5. No Academic Integrity Violations. Helping each other learn is encouraged. Sharing quiz answers, copying lab work, or completing assignments for others is not.
Enforcement: Minor rule violations may receive a warning. Toxic, hostile, or disrespectful behavior results in immediate permanent removal without warning. Harassment, threats, or academic integrity violations will also be reported to UTSA Student Conduct. This server is a privilege, not a right — it exists because students before you built a helpful community. Don't ruin it.
