Citations aren’t bureaucratic hoop-jumping — they’re credibility builders. A client can’t see your skills directly. They see your work. Every citation says: “I did the homework. You can trust this.”

Deduction: −10% per missing citation. 10 citations required. Zero citations = −100%. This requirement is non-negotiable.

  • 10+ citations — No deduction
  • 8 citations — −20%
  • 5 citations — −50%
  • 0 citations — −100%

How to Cite — Using Microsoft Word

Use Word’s built-in citation tools. Do not type references manually — Word automates formatting and bibliography generation.

  • Place your cursor where the citation belongs in the text
  • Go to References → Insert Citation → Add New Source
  • Fill in source details (author, title, URL, date accessed)
  • At document end: References → Bibliography → Insert Bibliography
  • Before submitting: Select All (Ctrl+A) then Update Fields (F9)

Style: APA format. Consistency matters. All citations in your document should use the same style.
