The Guiding Principle
"AI can help you understand and improve your work, but the work itself must be yours. Think of AI like a tutor, not a ghostwriter."

AI tools like ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, and others are part of the modern cybersecurity landscape. This policy defines how you can use them ethically and effectively in this course.

✓ Permitted Use
Explaining command syntax
Ask AI to explain what a command does or how flags work
Troubleshooting errors
Paste an error message and ask for debugging help
Understanding concepts
Ask AI to explain ideas from the textbook differently
Proofreading & grammar
Have AI check your report writing for clarity
Suggesting structure
Ask for feedback on report organization
✗ Prohibited Use
Writing report sections
AI cannot write your executive summaries or findings
Answering quiz questions
Quizzes verify you did the hands-on work
Generating fake screenshots
All evidence must come from your actual VM
Completing labs for you
You must run the tools yourself
Fabricating tool output
Real results only, even if they're incomplete
Why This Matters in Cybersecurity

In professional security work, your findings must be defensible and reproducible. If you tell a client they have a critical vulnerability, you need to prove it. AI-generated fabrications could lead to false positives (wasting client resources) or false negatives (missing real threats).

The skills you build here—running tools, interpreting output, documenting methodology—are exactly what employers and certifications test. Shortcuts now create gaps that will hurt you later.

Real Examples
✓ OK to Ask
"What does the -sV flag do in Nmap?"
"Why am I getting 'permission denied' when running this?"
"How should I organize my findings section?"
✗ Not OK to Ask
"Write an executive summary for my Nmap scan"
"Generate a vulnerability assessment report"
"What would Wireshark show if I captured DNS traffic?"
The Bottom Line: Use AI to understand concepts and tools. Don't use it to produce your deliverables. The goal is to make YOU a security professional, not to make you good at prompting AI.
