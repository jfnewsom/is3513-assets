This course is structured as a year-long consulting engagement.

You’re a Junior Analyst at NEXUS Security, a fictional Texas-based cybersecurity consulting firm. Over five modules you’ll work four real-world client engagements — Brazos Financial, Gulf Coast Healthcare, Alamo Industries, and LoneStar DevOps — spanning the full breadth of analyst work: reconnaissance, cryptography, network analysis, threat assessment, and risk management.

Every module produces an Engagement Packet — the same kind of deliverable a real consulting firm bills a client for. You’ll track your hours, document your methodology, and write client-facing reports backed by primary sources. The work is graded on the standard professional deliverables are held to, not on busywork.

This is a purple team course. You’ll learn offensive techniques (how attackers find weaknesses) and defensive thinking (how organizations close them) in parallel. The tools, frameworks, and reporting practices you build here are the same ones used in the field — and the same ones evaluated by UTSA’s cybersecurity competition teams. Learn more about NEXUS Security and meet the team you’ll work with.
