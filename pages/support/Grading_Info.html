How Your Work Is Evaluated

Your grade reflects professional competency, not just completion. Engagement Packets are graded in three tiers: additive scoring for the quality of what you submitted (Tier 1), documentation deductions for missing requirements (Tier 2), and a small bonus for work that exceeds the spec (Tier 3).

Engagement Packet Grading: Additive Scoring (Tier 1)

Your Engagement Packet is graded across two parts, weighted 40/60. Each section has its own sub-weight and is graded on a qualitative scale specific to that section. See the published Grading Standard for the full per-section level descriptors.

Part I — Internal Packet (40 points)

SectionWeightWhat We Evaluate
Timesheet5%Hours by unit, split internal training vs. client billable, justified, aligned with screenshot timestamps.
Runbook20%Man-page style per-tool entries. Could the next analyst reproduce your engagement from this alone?
Analyst Reflection15%Honest, specific reflection. AI usage disclosed and validated, or N/A if not used.

Part II — Client Deliverable (60 points)

SectionWeightWhat We Evaluate
Executive Summary12%One page max. Risk posture, severity counts, top recommendations with business framing.
Methodology7%Brief client-facing narrative of your engagement approach.
Findings22%Numbered, severity-rated, evidence-backed findings ordered by severity.
Risk Analysis11%Per-finding likelihood and impact, reasoned through the client’s business context.
Recommendations8%Prioritized — Monday-morning fixes vs. next-quarter budget items.

Cover Page, Table of Contents, and Appendix are mechanical requirements — they are either present or not, and their absence is handled in the Documentation Deductions section below.

Documentation Deductions (Tier 2)
−10% per missing citation (10 required)
−5% per missing or unverifiable screenshot (20 required for Modules 1–4; Module 5 floor in the lab body)
−20% if the Appendix is missing
−10% if the Executive Summary exceeds one page
−5% if the Cover Page is missing
−5% if the Table of Contents is missing or hand-typed
−5% if Word fields aren’t updated (visible “Error! Reference source not found” or stale figure numbers)
Combined deduction cap is −100%. Citations must meet the source quality standard — see the Citations page.

Engagement Packet Bonus (Tier 3)

Up to +10% for work that exceeds the spec. Examples: a non-required tool documented to runbook standard, a finding that shows analyst-level judgment beyond what the lab scenario requires, or documentation craftsmanship that materially improves the deliverable. Bonuses are awarded sparingly and never as compensation for Tier 1 quality gaps.

Think of drops as built-in sick days

Just like at work, sick days are not unlimited. Depending on how long you've been with the company, you get more — but in this class, you only have one per category. Take more than one day at work and it comes out of your check in the form of forfeited wages. Take more than one drop here and it comes out of your GPA in the form of forfeited grades.

block
Late Work Policy

No late work accepted. If an emergency makes you miss a deadline, your one drop in that category absorbs it. Plan accordingly — drops are scarce, so save them for real emergencies.
