Your Colleagues at NEXUS Security

These are your colleagues at NEXUS Security. You’ll hear from them throughout the semester — in lab callouts, mentor quotes, and guidance sections. They’re fictional, but the lessons they teach are real.

Marcus Chen — Founder & Principal Consultant

Former NSA analyst turned private sector. Marcus founded NEXUS after 15 years in government cybersecurity. He sets the strategic direction for engagements and handles executive-level client relationships. You won’t hear from him often — but when you do, listen.

“The executive summary is the most important page in your report. Make sure a CEO can hand it to their board and look confident.”

Maya Rodriguez — Senior Penetration Tester

Red team specialist with a talent for finding what others miss. Maya leads offensive engagements and mentors junior analysts on methodology. She’s methodical, technical, and pushes you to dig deeper. Her training sessions are challenging but fair.

“Start wide, then go deep. Port scan first, then investigate interesting services. Don’t tunnel-vision on the first finding.”

Carmen Vega — Junior Security Analyst

Your peer at NEXUS — she started six months before you. Carmen handles OSINT research and client prep. She’s been where you are, made the same mistakes, and learned from them. She’ll share research openly and warn you about common pitfalls.

“I forgot to screenshot my nmap scan before moving on. Had to re-run everything. Save as you go — trust me.”

Derek Mitchell — Security Operations Lead

Blue team specialist who monitors and defends NEXUS client environments. Derek oversees SOC workflows, incident response procedures, and defensive tooling. He’s the one who builds the playbooks that stop the attacks Maya finds.

“Every red team finding is a detection opportunity. When you find something, my first question is: would we have seen this in the logs?”

Maria Delgado, PhD — Security Compliance Specialist

Bridges the gap between technical findings and regulatory requirements. Maria translates NEXUS engagements into HIPAA, PCI-DSS, and CMMC compliance language. She’s the one who makes sure your report recommendations actually map to what the client’s auditors will check.

“Cryptography isn’t magic — it’s math. And math done wrong is worse than no math at all because it gives false confidence.”
